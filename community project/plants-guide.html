Vegetables for Pocket Gardens

Perfect vegetables to grow in containers and small spaces

Tomatoes

Tomatoes are one of the most rewarding vegetables to grow in containers. They produce abundant fruit in a small space.

Growing Guide
Timeline
Care Tips
Sunlight: 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily
Water: Keep soil consistently moist but not soggy
Temperature: 18-29°C (65-85°F)
Container: 5-gallon pot (minimum) with drainage holes
Varieties: Cherry, Roma, Beefsteak (determinate varieties best for containers)

Growing Timeline (90-120 days total)

Day 1-7
Day 21-28
Day 40-50
Day 60-80
Day 90-120

Day 1-7: Seed germination
Day 21-28: Seedling with true leaves
Day 40-50: Flowering begins
Day 60-80: First fruits appear
Day 90-120: Harvest time

Care Instructions

  • Stake or cage plants for support as they grow
  • Prune suckers (side shoots) for better air circulation
  • Feed with balanced fertilizer every 2 weeks
  • Water at the base to avoid leaf diseases
  • Harvest when fruits are firm and fully colored

Troubleshooting

  • Yellow leaves: Possible overwatering or nutrient deficiency
  • Blossom end rot: Calcium deficiency, maintain consistent watering
  • Cracked fruits: Inconsistent watering, maintain even moisture
  • Curling leaves: Heat stress or pest infestation

Chili Peppers

Chili peppers are compact plants that produce abundantly in containers and add spice to your cooking.

Growing Guide
Timeline
Care Tips
Sunlight: 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily
Water: Allow soil to dry slightly between watering
Temperature: 21-32°C (70-90°F)
Container: 3-gallon pot with drainage holes
Varieties: Thai, Cayenne, Jalapeño, Bird's Eye

Growing Timeline (80-120 days total)

Day 1-10
Day 20-30
Day 35-50
Day 60-80
Day 80-120

Day 1-10: Seed germination
Day 20-30: Seedling with true leaves
Day 35-50: Flowering begins
Day 60-80: Green peppers form
Day 80-120: Peppers ripen to final color

Care Instructions

  • Provide support for taller varieties
  • Reduce watering once fruits begin to form
  • Feed with low-nitrogen, high-phosphorus fertilizer
  • Harvest regularly to encourage more production
  • Pinch off early flowers to establish stronger plants

Troubleshooting

  • Dropping flowers: Temperature stress or inconsistent watering
  • Stunted growth: Nutrient deficiency or root binding
  • Curling leaves: Aphid infestation or heat stress
  • Small fruits: Insufficient pollination, gently shake plants

Spinach

Spinach is a fast-growing leafy green that's perfect for small spaces and provides nutritious harvests.

Growing Guide
Timeline
Care Tips
Sunlight: 3-4 hours of direct sunlight or partial shade
Water: Keep soil consistently moist
Temperature: 10-24°C (50-75°F)
Container: Wide, shallow container (6" deep)
Varieties: Bloomsdale, New Zealand, Malabar (heat-tolerant)

Growing Timeline (40-50 days total)

Day 1-5
Day 10-15
Day 20-30
Day 30-40
Day 40-50

Day 1-5: Seed germination
Day 10-15: First true leaves appear
Day 20-30: Young leaves ready for baby spinach harvest
Day 30-40: Full-sized leaves developing
Day 40-50: Full harvest (before bolting in warm weather)

Care Instructions

  • Sow seeds successively every 2-3 weeks for continuous harvest
  • Harvest outer leaves first to extend production
  • Provide afternoon shade in hot weather
  • Apply balanced liquid fertilizer every 2-3 weeks
  • Grow during cooler months to prevent bolting

Troubleshooting

  • Bolting: Heat stress, plant in cooler seasons or use heat-tolerant varieties
  • Yellow leaves: Overwatering or nitrogen deficiency
  • Holes in leaves: Leaf miners or slugs, remove affected leaves
  • Slow growth: Insufficient nutrients, apply balanced fertilizer

Herbs for Pocket Gardens

Aromatic herbs that thrive in small containers and add flavor to your cooking

Mint

Mint is a vigorous herb that's perfect for containers and adds refreshing flavor to drinks and dishes.

Growing Guide
Timeline
Care Tips
Sunlight: Partial shade to full sun (4-6 hours)
Water: Keep soil consistently moist
Temperature: 18-26°C (65-80°F)
Container: 8-10" pot with drainage (contained to prevent spreading)
Varieties: Spearmint, Peppermint, Chocolate Mint, Apple Mint

Growing Timeline (60-90 days from cutting)

Day 1-7
Day 14-21
Day 30-45
Day 45-60
Day 60-90

Day 1-7: Cutting develops roots
Day 14-21: New growth appears
Day 30-45: Plant becomes established
Day 45-60: First light harvest possible
Day 60-90: Full, regular harvesting

Care Instructions

  • Grow in a dedicated container as mint spreads aggressively
  • Harvest regularly to encourage bushy growth
  • Pinch flower buds to maintain flavor
  • Divide plants every 1-2 years to rejuvenate
  • Fertilize lightly; mint prefers moderate nutrients

Troubleshooting

  • Leggy growth: Insufficient light, move to brighter location
  • Rust spots: Fungal disease, improve air circulation
  • Wilting: Underwatering or root rot from overwatering
  • Pale leaves: Nutrient deficiency, apply balanced fertilizer

Curry Leaves

Curry leaf plants add authentic flavor to Indian dishes and can thrive as a potted plant in warm conditions.

Growing Guide
Timeline
Care Tips
Sunlight: 4-6 hours of bright, indirect light
Water: Allow top inch of soil to dry between watering
Temperature: 20-35°C (68-95°F)
Container: 12" deep pot with good drainage
Varieties: Murraya koenigii (standard curry leaf)

Growing Timeline (1-2 years for established plant)

Month 1
Month 3
Month 6
Month 9
Year 1-2

Month 1: Seedling establishment
Month 3: Young plant with multiple leaves
Month 6: Growing plant with branching
Month 9: Limited harvesting possible
Year 1-2: Mature plant with regular harvests

Care Instructions

  • Use well-draining soil with added sand or perlite
  • Protect from cold drafts and temperatures below 15°C (59°F)
  • Fertilize monthly with balanced organic fertilizer
  • Prune to maintain bushy growth and height
  • Bring indoors during winter in colder climates

Troubleshooting

  • Leaf drop: Temperature stress or overwatering
  • Yellow leaves: Nutrient deficiency or overwatering
  • Leggy growth: Insufficient light
  • Pests: Spider mites or scale insects, treat with neem oil

Basil

Basil is a fragrant herb that grows quickly in containers and is essential for Italian and Thai cooking.

Growing Guide
Timeline
Care Tips
Sunlight: 6-8 hours of direct sunlight
Water: Keep soil consistently moist but not soggy
Temperature: 18-30°C (65-86°F)
Container: 6-8" pot with drainage holes
Varieties: Genovese, Thai, Holy Basil, Lemon Basil

Growing Timeline (60-80 days total)

Day 1-5
Day 10-15
Day 20-30
Day 40-60
Day 60-80

Day 1-5: Seed germination
Day 10-15: First true leaves appear
Day 20-30: Young plant with multiple leaf pairs
Day 40-60: Begin harvesting outer leaves
Day 60-80: Full-sized plant with regular harvests

Care Instructions

  • Pinch off flower buds to extend leaf production
  • Harvest from the top to encourage bushy growth
  • Fertilize lightly every 4-6 weeks
  • Provide good air circulation to prevent disease
  • Harvest in the morning for best flavor

Troubleshooting

  • Black spots: Fungal disease, improve air circulation
  • Yellowing leaves: Overwatering or nutrient deficiency
  • Leggy growth: Insufficient light
  • Wilting: Underwatering or heat stress

Fruits for Pocket Gardens

Compact fruit varieties that can thrive in containers

Strawberries

Strawberries are perfect for hanging baskets or window boxes and produce sweet fruits in small spaces.

Growing Guide
Timeline
Care Tips
Sunlight: 6-8 hours of direct sunlight
Water: Keep soil consistently moist
Temperature: 15-26°C (60-80°F)
Container: Hanging basket, strawberry pot, or 8" deep container
Varieties: Alpine, Day-neutral, Everbearing varieties

Growing Timeline (1 year for full production)

Month 1
Month 2-3
Month 4-5
Month 6-8
Year 1

Month 1: Plant establishment
Month 2-3: Leaf and runner development
Month 4-5: Flowering begins
Month 6-8: First fruits (limited harvest)
Year 1: Full production cycle

Care Instructions

  • Use rich, slightly acidic soil with good drainage
  • Remove runners to focus energy on fruit production
  • Place straw around plants to keep fruits clean
  • Fertilize with balanced fertilizer before flowering
  • Replace plants every 2-3 years for best production

Troubleshooting

  • Small fruits: Insufficient pollination or nutrients
  • Rotting fruits: Too much moisture, improve air circulation
  • Leaf spots: Fungal disease, avoid overhead watering
  • Pest damage: Birds or insects, consider netting

Dwarf Citrus

Dwarf citrus trees can thrive in large containers and produce fragrant flowers and fruits year after year.

Growing Guide
Timeline
Care Tips
Sunlight: 8-12 hours of bright light, preferably direct sun
Water: Allow top inch of soil to dry between watering
Temperature: 18-29°C (65-85°F)
Container: 15-20" pot with excellent drainage
Varieties: Dwarf Lemon, Lime, Kumquat, Calamondin

Growing Timeline (2-3 years for fruit production)

Month 1-3
Month 6-12
Year 1-2
Year 2-3
Year 3+

Month 1-3: Plant establishment
Month 6-12: Vegetative growth
Year 1-2: First flowering may occur
Year 2-3: First fruits may develop
Year 3+: Regular fruit production

Care Instructions

  • Use citrus-specific potting mix with good drainage
  • Fertilize with citrus fertilizer every 6-8 weeks during growing season
  • Mist leaves regularly to increase humidity
  • Move indoors when temperatures drop below 10°C (50°F)
  • Hand-pollinate flowers with a small brush for better fruit set

Troubleshooting

  • Leaf drop: Temperature stress or improper watering
  • Yellow leaves: Nutrient deficiency, especially iron
  • No flowering: Insufficient light or nutrients
  • Fruit drop: Inconsistent watering or temperature fluctuations

Blueberries

Dwarf blueberry varieties can be grown in containers and provide nutritious berries with beautiful foliage.

Growing Guide
Timeline
Care Tips
Sunlight: 6-8 hours of direct sunlight
Water: Keep soil consistently moist but not soggy
Temperature: 15-26°C (60-80°F)
Container: 18" pot with excellent drainage
Varieties: Dwarf Tophat, Northsky, Sunshine Blue

Growing Timeline (2-3 years for full production)

Month 1-3
Month 6-12
Year 1
Year 2
Year 3+

Month 1-3: Plant establishment
Month 6-12: Vegetative growth
Year 1: Limited flowering and fruiting
Year 2: Increased fruit production
Year 3+: Full fruit production

Care Instructions

  • Use acidic soil (pH 4.5-5.5) with peat moss and pine bark
  • Water with rainwater or acidified water if possible
  • Fertilize with acid-loving plant fertilizer
  • Prune in late winter to remove old wood
  • Protect from birds with netting when fruits ripen

Troubleshooting

  • Yellow leaves: pH too high, acidify soil
  • No fruit: Insufficient pollination, plant two varieties
  • Leaf spots: Fungal disease, improve air circulation
  • Small fruits: Insufficient water or nutrients
